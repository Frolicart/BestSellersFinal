Best Selling Games

By Benjamin Russo

questions formed on the data

How many best sellers were before the 2000’s?
This set of data was separated based on a color coordination, where the color itself was automated but the count was manually done as the data set wasn’t immense and doesn’t fluctuate all that often. Everything in column F and G was highlighted purple to denote which game was released before January 1st, 2000. 

What year had the most high selling games?
 Data was separated into 2 columns manually, one side (F) was the month, day and year, the other partner column was separated into years to help the conditional formatting do its job. The formula was generated by rules already present within google sheets and formatted to the right side of the sheets data. 

Findings

Before the 2000's there were 9 games that made the list, Nintendo had 7 games out of 9 that made the list. Namco and Sonic Team were the other 2 companies that followed them up in the years before. Where it does say "Game Freak" as a developer is also considered to be under Nintendo because "Pokemon" is a game title owned by Nintendo. 

In 2006, the most common year on the list, Nintendo also dominated that year as well. With titles like Wii Sports, Wii Play, Pokemon Pearl/Diamond and New Super Mario Bros., they had a dominant year with 4 out of 5 games being top sellers during this year. The only other company to have had a top seller this year was Electronic Arts Mobile with their release of Tetris (which has the 3rd top spot on the list.)

Based on the 2 questions proposed, I came to the conclusion that Nintendo not only had multiple high selling games released within one year, but they also dominated before the 2000's with their classic releases that remain on the top sellers list to this day.

bias in machine learning

I have a background in machine learning bias, and that pertains to the field of art and its sub-categories. To see how machine learning affects not only the art world in a vast majority of ways, but also the criminal justice system is astonishing. Heavy reliance on these new algorithms can result in very skewed results and actions taken by those acting on said data sets. That said, a larger and more public data set can help to improve MLAs overall but with a greater deal of human intervention at the ready.
